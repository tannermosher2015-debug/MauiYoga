Oncology & Chronic Care

For the days your body
has been through enough.

Yoga therapy and patient advocacy for people in cancer treatment or living with chronic illness — led by a nurse who has sat on both sides of the bed.

Christina spent her clinical career in oncology and chronic-care nursing. She knows the fatigue, the ports and the scars, the days you can barely move and the days you need to feel like yourself again. She also knows yoga therapy from the inside — how breath, gentle movement, and stillness can give some control back when so much feels out of your hands.

This work sits alongside your medical team, never in place of it. It’s not a cure and it makes no promises. It’s a careful, human practice — scaled to exactly what you can do today, and changed the moment that changes.

A gentler practice Vertical 3:4, restful

Why a nurse changes everything

She reads the room and the chart.

A typical instructor has to guess what’s safe for you. Christina doesn’t. She understands treatment timelines, blood counts, fall and infection risk, neuropathy, surgical precautions — the real constraints your body is under. That knowledge is the difference between a class you have to be brave to attend and a room you can simply rest in.

Ways she can help

Support, shaped to you.

Gentle yoga therapy

Breath, restorative shapes, and slow movement adapted to your treatment stage — in private or, when you’re ready, in a small supported setting.

Nurse patient advocacy

A knowledgeable ally to help you understand information, prepare questions for your team, and feel less alone in the system.

Breath & nervous-system care

Simple breathing and meditation practices for anxiety, sleep, nausea, and the long waiting that illness brings.

Care for caregivers

Support for the family members and partners who carry so much — because they need a safe room too.

“You don’t have to be strong here. That’s my job. You just have to breathe.”
Christina Martini · RN & yoga therapist

No pressure, no commitment

Start with a conversation.

If you or someone you love is in treatment, reach out and simply talk to Christina. She’ll help you find what’s safe and what might help — at your pace, on your terms.
