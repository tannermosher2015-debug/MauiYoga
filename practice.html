Two ways into the room

New here? Start with this.

Ashtanga has a vocabulary. Here’s the only part you need before your first morning.

Mysore self-practice

A.M.

You move through the sequence at your own breath while Christina circulates — adjusting, teaching the next posture only when you’re ready for it. It looks advanced. It’s actually the gentlest on-ramp there is, because nothing is rushed and nothing is performed.

  • Learn one posture at a time
  • Hands-on, individual attention in a group room
  • Best for beginners and seasoned practitioners alike

Led class

WKLY

The traditional counted class: Christina calls the breath and the group moves together. A steady, meditative rhythm — and the best way to learn the proper count once you know the postures.

  • Guided, breath-by-breath
  • Builds stamina and timing
  • Modifications offered throughout

1:1 Yoga Therapy

When a group room isn’t the right room.

Private sessions are built entirely around you — your history, your injuries, your goals. Christina draws on both the lineage and her clinical training to give you a practice that protects what needs protecting and rebuilds the rest.

People come for recovery after surgery or injury, for chronic pain, for anxiety and sleep, for getting back to movement after a long time away, or simply to be taught carefully, without a crowd.
