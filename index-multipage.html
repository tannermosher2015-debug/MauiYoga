The difference

Most teachers can tell you the pose. She can read your chart.

Christina has spent three decades in two disciplines at once — the Ashtanga lineage of Manju Jois, and oncology and chronic-care nursing. She doesn’t choose between them. She teaches yoga the way a good clinician works: meet the body that’s actually in front of you, never the one in the brochure.

That means a practice that holds up through injury, illness, pregnancy, treatment, and age — not in spite of those things, but designed around them. You don’t have to be flexible, fearless, or twenty-five. You have to be willing to breathe.
